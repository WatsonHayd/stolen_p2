Disguised Theft of your
private information

In the world today, the internet is the number one source of information. With very little effort, using a device we can find the information that week seek at just a click of a button. But in the act of searching for information we are also providing information, personal information that you may not want people seeing. When you visit a website, that site can gather information about you and store it away without your knowledge. STOLEN is designed in much the same way, however STOLEN doesn’t hide your information away; STOLEN gathers that information and sends it right back to you, so you can see exactly what the internet knows about you

What is STOLEN and
who is it for?

STOLEN is a web browser extension that monitors your web activity. All data that STOLEN gathers is not sent externally, all information is your own and STOLEN treats it that way. Using the same algorithms that many sites like social media use to store your information, STOLEN will report back to the user listing the personal information that the site has stolen off you. Going on to Facebook to change your profile Bio? Using Google to find what time that store is open until? Sites like these take that information to piece together who you are without you knowing.

STOLEN is a way for the user to understand what happens behind the scenes and shows them the importance of personal privacy when using the internet.

STOLEN is built for any user of the internet that cares about their own private information. A likely audience age is from mid-teens and up, at these ages the use of social media become more common in their everyday lives, so knowing what they are sharing with the world should be monitored with caution. As people get older and use the internet more and more, stolen personal information get larger and larger too, keeping up with what you have shared is pretty important so STOLEN can be used at all ages young or old.

Visual Direction

Colour Pallet:

STOLENS visual and art direction should convey the idea that it is trying to push. The internet can be a dark and scary place even if these websites we use every day made to look appealing. STOLEN will have a simplistic look with contrasting black/Gray and white colours. The contrast in colour is made to make STOLEN look like a sort of saviour amongst the darkness, hence the white STOLEN logo.

STOLEN uses a very plain colour pallet, made up of mostly just Gray and White. The colour codes that the design follows are specifically:
Gray = R-56 G-56 B-56
White= R-255 G-255 B-255

Typography/Font:

As well as the contrasting colours, the text should be much the same. A balance of simplistic, slender text paired with big and blocky text. The fonts used will be:

IMPACT - A big a blocky font used for titles and pushing ideas forth (Used in logo)

Lucida Sans - light text for larger paragraphs and information

The Logo

This logo uses STOLENs main font choice of: Impact. However the O in STOLEN is replaced by a graphic of a keylock. This is to represent putting a lock and safekeeping your data, of which STOLEN is aiming to achieve
